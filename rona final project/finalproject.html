Blue crab blues
The harvest can't go on like this much longer.

by Rona Kobell




Blue crabs are the dominant fishery in the Chesapeake Bay now, and the crustaceans are showing they can't take the pressure. Since 1993, harvests have been dwindling, with several points requiring Maryland, Virginia, and the organization that controls the Potomac River's fishery to take action. Harvest restrictions are never popular, and they tend to hit the Deal Island area hardest. But they have likely saved the population from free fall.
